## Overview

Create a fresh Canva Instagram portrait design for **The Lincoln** for **Worth It Melbourne**. The design should feel like a premium, casual Melbourne food-critic recommendation, not a generic restaurant ad.

The core story: **Historic Carlton pub comfort, from 12pm until sold out.**

Primary viewer takeaway within 2 seconds: **WORTH IT FOR SUNDAY ROAST — Historic Carlton pub comfort, from 12pm until sold out.**.

## Colors

Use a warm, appetite-driven palette inspired by Asian-fusion dining, cocktails, dumplings, timber interiors, night-out energy, and Melbourne CBD hospitality.

- **Primary (#17130F)**: deep espresso/ink for strong editorial contrast.
- **Tertiary (#B8422E)**: warm red accent for CTA, energy, and appetite appeal.
- **Neutral (#F7F1E8)**: warm paper/cream background for premium editorial softness.
- **Accent (#D8A24A)**: muted gold for small highlights, dividers, or badges.

Avoid sterile white-on-stock-photo layouts. Avoid random bright colors that do not match the restaurant mood.

## Restaurant visual identity synthesis

Use these cues to generate original imagery and design language that feels close to the business without uploading or copying their photos:

- **Brand style:** Refined urban cellar-door, premium but understated. Elegant uppercase wordmark energy, boutique hospitality, not flashy nightlife.
- **Colour mood:** Deep black, walnut brown, espresso, charcoal, cream/ivory, warm amber/gold lighting, and subtle wine-burgundy accents.
- **Typography feel:** Sophisticated tall editorial serif or refined high-contrast display face for the restaurant name, paired with clean modern sans-serif support copy.
- **Imagery to generate:** Generate original upscale wine-bar/cellar-door imagery: wine bottles, stemmed glasses, wine pour or tasting moment, polished tabletop, warm bar light, subtle city/park evening ambience. Do not copy official photos exactly.
- **Materials and textures:** Dark timber, wood grain veneer, glass reflections, matte black finishes, soft amber light, small brass or metallic details.
- **Composition cues:** Warm low-light crops, natural social-post energy, tactile details, relaxed premium mood, useful and saveable rather than staged or corporate.
- **Avoid:** Avoid generic cocktail stock, nightclub neon, beer-pub cues, rustic vineyard clichés, and fake menu/price claims.

## Typography

Use bold, mobile-readable typography. The restaurant name must be immediately legible.

Recommended hierarchy:

1. Eyebrow: WORTH IT MELBOURNE
2. Hero: WORTH IT FOR SUNDAY ROAST
3. Hook: Historic Carlton pub comfort, from 12pm until sold out.
4. Score: 8.7/10
5. Verdict: Worth it for a slow Sunday pub lunch, especially if you want comfort food without beige pub energy. Book ahead and go early before it sells out.
6. Address: 91 Cardigan St, Carlton VIC 3053
7. Support: Why go: proper Sunday roast / What I’d order: the rotating roast / Damage: check current menu / My score: 8.7/10 / Verdict: book ahead, go early
8. CTA: Save this before your next Melbourne Sunday

Short social copy is preferred over dense text. If space is tight, preserve hero, hook, and CTA first.

## Layout

Instagram portrait, 1080x1350.

Recommended structure:

- Large official food/venue image as the emotional anchor.
- Editorial text panel or overlay with high contrast.
- Hero restaurant name in the upper or central third.
- Hook near the hero name.
- CTA in a clear pill or badge.
- Keep enough safe margins for mobile crop.

Do not split the restaurant name awkwardly unless it still reads instantly as “The Lincoln”.

## Elevation & Depth

Use soft editorial layering: warm card surfaces, subtle shadows, image masks, or magazine-style collage. Avoid fake app UI, overdone mockups, or decorative elements that distract from the food and hook.

## Shapes

Use rounded editorial cards and pill CTAs sparingly. The shapes should feel premium and intentional, not template-like.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Make the design feel specifically like The Lincoln.
- Generate original Canva imagery that reflects the restaurant's visual identity.
- Make WORTH IT FOR SUNDAY ROAST the clearest text.
- Communicate the hook: Historic Carlton pub comfort, from 12pm until sold out..
- Include a visible save/share CTA.
- Keep it readable on a phone at small size.

### Don't

- Do not use placeholder handles like @reallygreatsite.
- Do not invent claims like “best”, “#1”, “award-winning”, or “must-visit”.
- Do not make it feel like generic stock food content.
- Do not upload or directly reuse official restaurant images unless Juan explicitly asks.
- Do not bury the restaurant name.
- Do not overfill the design with caption-length text.
